Interests

Benjamin Franklin once quoted "Early to bed and early to rise, makes a man healthy, wealthy, and wise,". I like to believe that pursuing one's interests and living life in a jubilant manner should produce a similar effect. That being said, I myself pursue a number of strange and often business related hobbies including: skiing; driving my car; reproducing medieval armor; establishing and developing a quite legitimate independent nation state; picking the ponies, when I'm near the track; exercising my second amendment right; watching, "Top Gear" and, "The Grand Tour", my two favorite TV shows; scheming of new, somewhat shady business ventures, nothing dangerous or harmful, usually just selling walking sticks or collecting loans where I'm not supposed to; and adding to my ever growing collection of antique toys, coins, etc.

Of the activities mentioned above, one of my more interesting endevers certainly is the "reproduction of medieval armor". Having been exposed, first hand, to the wonders of the old world, during a family trip to Ireland when I was eight years old, I've since been deeply interested in medieval history and warfare. That being said, I only ever enjoyed history in the museum or in a book until a couple years ago when I was introduced to the idea of actually making medieval armor. Having invested some time watching youtube videos on making chainmail and money to acquire the proper tools and wire, I began producing small scraps of 12th century garments before school and in the evening. The process, though simple, is a real art form and extremely time consuming. That being said I was obsessed by the patterns and play and weight of the armor. Long story schort, I ended up producing a shirt of chainmail the end of my sophomore year of high school. By the end of my junior year, I had produced an entire suit of armor which you can see me wearing above. I haven't made much of anything in the past 6 months however, anything made of chainmail is one of those "months long" projects though, I wont hesitate to pick up the pliers and wire when my next idea hits me.
